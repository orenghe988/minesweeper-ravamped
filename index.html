Help

Minesweeper is a game where mines are hidden behind a set amount of squares in a grid.
Some squares are empty, while others have mines behind them.
Every square in the grid can be stepped on, revealing what lies behind it.

The goal of the game is to reveal all squares that do not have mines behind them.
If the player steps on a square with a mine behind it, they would lose the game.

To help the player, squares that don't have mines behind them, but are 'touching' squares that do (directly adjacent vertically, horizontally, or diagonally), will show a number behind them (only when stepped on).
The number (1 - 8), indicates the exact amount of bomb squares that touch this square.

If the player has come to a decision that a bomb lies behind a certain square, they should flag it by right-clicking on the square. Flagging the square would prevent it from being stepped on, and decrement the remaining bombs counter by 1.
It's important to know, that flags can also be placed on empty squares, while also preventing them from being stepped on, and decrementing the remaining bombs counter by 1.

In their first moves, the player should try to step on a square that is both safe and not touching any unsafe squares. This would open even more squares around it, giving the player more info about bombs around.

Good luck!
